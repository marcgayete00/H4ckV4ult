      "Follina" is the nickname for a critical remote code execution (RCE) vulnerability in Microsoft Windows, officially tracked as CVE-2022-30190. It was first publicly discovered in late May 2022 and allows attackers to take control of a target system by tricking users into opening a specially crafted document, often through phishing campaigns.
